The officially designated thread
standard for garden hose connectors in
the United States and Canada is known
as NH, which stands for "National Hose”.

Hose coupling specs are standardized by law.
For example, there are NFPA and ASME-ANSI
standards for automotive, appliance, or fire hose
fittings. ‘NH’ designates full-form threads produced
by directly cutting solid metal, which is the highest
quality standard for this type of use, as opposed
to threads produced by rolling thin material and
designated ‘NHR’, such as are typically found
on lower quality fixtures.

Please Note

Know your intended irrigation purpose
with this device. A bubbler is not a sprayer
that disperses water widely across an area.
A bubbler’s basic yet vitally useful purpose is to
simply break the force of high-pressure plumbing
flow so it instead becomes a gentle, localized soak.
Rather than flowing from every pore of the entire
bubbler head all at once, water burbles along the
path of least resistance from those perforations
directly nearest the spot you’re irrigating, fo
a localized, soft yet voluminous soaking.
This means it’s working properly,
as designed & intended.

Metals react electrolytically with one another
in the presence of dampness, which can cause
dissolved minerals in tapwater to mineralize screw-
threads over time. It’s wise to lube your bubbler’s
threads (petroleum jelly or most any kind of oil will
do) if you plan to leave the bubbler attached to
metal hose couplings over a prolonged period.
